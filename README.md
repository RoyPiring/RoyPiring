Cloud systems where **constraints** and **tradeoffs** are explicit—not afterthoughts.

Designing and operating cloud systems where **constraints matter**. Most of what shows up in production traces back to whether the tradeoffs were understood **before** anything was built—not after.

I work with **small and mid-sized teams** to pressure-test technical decisions early: architecture, observability, security, and bringing in newer tech (including AI) in a **controlled** way—clear docs, explored edge cases, less to unwind later.

Outside of work I still evaluate **newer tools and patterns**, but only where they earn their place: fit real constraints, avoid needless complexity, and hold up when systems scale.

## How I work

**Before building:** what problem, what constraints, what failure looks like, what tradeoffs you’re taking.

**With teams:** architecture, observability, security, and pragmatic AI—docs, design exploration, repetitive ops—while keeping **control, cost, and reliability** in view as things grow.

**AI in the loop:** drafting and refining system documentation, exploring design options and edge cases, and taking repetitive operational work off human plates—**when** the guardrails are clear.

**This GitHub:** decisions, tradeoffs, and patterns that still make sense under real-world constraints—not slide-deck architecture.
